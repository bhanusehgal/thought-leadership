## Writing Style Guide

### Source Basis
Derived from:
`https://medium.com/@bhanusehgal25/suspicious-transactions-and-aml-alerts-dont-just-create-compliance-risk-they-contain-early-e3bcbd8b01f4`

Observed metrics from extracted text:
- Sentence length: mean about 16 words, median 15, typical range 11-19.
- Paragraph size: usually 2-4 sentences; occasional single-sentence pivot paragraph.
- Rhetorical questions: none observed.
- First-person usage: none observed.

### Core Style Rules
- Keep sentence length mostly in the 12-20 word range.
- Use short-to-medium paragraphs (2-4 sentences), with occasional one-line impact paragraph.
- Prefer declarative statements over rhetorical questions.
- Use first-person sparingly; default to neutral institutional tone.
- Emphasize causal mechanics, not abstract commentary.

### Structural Template To Replicate
1. Thesis/problem statement that challenges a common industry assumption.
2. Why the existing operating model worked historically.
3. Why that assumption fails under current transaction speed/complexity.
4. Concrete behavior patterns or failure modes.
5. Integrated framework/reframe that connects previously siloed functions.
6. Implication-focused conclusion with operational takeaway.

### Rhetorical Framing Style
- Lead with a precise claim, then narrow to mechanism.
- Use contrast transitions: "correct yet incomplete," "not X, but Y," "individually vs collectively."
- Reframe from compliance-only interpretation to liquidity/system interpretation where applicable.

### Sharp Reframing Rule
Each article must contain 2-3 sharp reframes (precise, surgical statements that clarify misconceptions without being political or aggressive).

### Examples Of Sharp Reframing (from source article)
- "That assumption is correct yet incomplete."
- "The goal is not to replace AML systems or liquidity models, but to connect them."
- "Stability can no longer be assumed. It must be measured at the point of transaction."

### Vocabulary Profile
- High technical density with readable flow.
- Preferred terms: liquidity stress, transaction behavior, balance sheet impact, model assumptions, propagation effects.
- Use plain language for transitions; keep technical nouns precise.

### Words/Phrases To Avoid (Buzzwords / Weakening Language)
- "Revolutionary"
- "Game-changing"
- "Disruptive synergy"
- "Paradigm shift"
- "Leverage AI" (without concrete mechanism)
- "Digital transformation" (as filler)
- "Thought leadership"
- "Unprecedented opportunity"

### Directness Calibration
- Be firm when correcting assumptions.
- Avoid aggressive framing, ideology, and culture-war language.
- Prefer surgical clarification to broad criticism.

### Compliance Boundaries
- No political commentary.
- No investment advice.
- No employer/client disclosure.
